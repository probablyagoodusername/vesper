[BREATHING: Deep diaphragmatic breathing — slow inhale through nose 5 counts, exhale through mouth 7 counts, 3 cycles]

Good morning.

Let's ground before we go.

Place your feet flat on the floor if you're sitting, or press your palms together if you're lying down. Feel the contact. The pressure. Something solid.

[5s pause]

Now breathe with me. In through your nose for five... two... three... four... five. And out through your mouth for seven... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[3s pause]

Again. In... two... three... four... five. And out... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[3s pause]

One more. In... two... three... four... five. Out... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

[5s pause]

Good. You're here. Present. Grounded.

Now listen to this. God speaking to Joshua, who was about to lead an entire nation into unknown territory, terrified, standing in Moses' shadow:

"Have I not commanded you? Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; do not be discouraged, for the Lord your God will be with you wherever you go." Joshua 1, verse 9.

Notice the word. Commanded. Not suggested. Not "it would be nice if you could be brave." Commanded. Which means courage is not a personality trait you either have or don't. It's a decision. Something you do, not something you feel.

[10s pause]

And then Deuteronomy, same message, different moment: "Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid or terrified because of them, for the Lord your God goes with you; he will never leave you nor forsake you." Deuteronomy 31, verse 6.

Two different books. Two different speakers. The same command. Be courageous. Because you are not alone.

[10s pause]

So what are you avoiding? Name it. The conversation you keep rehearsing but never starting. The email sitting in drafts. The decision that feels too heavy. The doctor's appointment. The apology. The boundary.

Name one thing.

[15s pause]

Now feel the resistance. Where does it live in your body? The tightness in your chest? The heaviness in your arms? The knot in your stomach? That's fear... wearing a costume called "later." Wearing a costume called "I'm not ready." Wearing a costume called "maybe next week."

[10s pause]

Here's what the research shows: courage and fear are not opposites. They coexist. Every act of bravery in human history was performed by someone who was afraid. The feeling of fear is not evidence that you shouldn't do something. It's often evidence that it matters.

[10s pause]

So let's do something with this.

Put your hand on your chest. Feel your heartbeat. It's steady. It's been steady through every hard thing you've ever done. Your body already knows how to carry you through fear. You just have to let it.

[10s pause]

Now close your eyes if they aren't already. See yourself doing the thing you named. Not the catastrophe version your anxiety plays on repeat. The real version.

See yourself walking into it with steady breath. Your shoulders are back, not braced. Your hands are open, not clenched. See yourself speaking clearly, even if your voice shakes a little. See yourself in that room. That office. That hospital. That conversation. That mirror.

[15s pause]

Now see yourself on the other side of it. Feel the relief. The lightness. That quiet dignity of having faced something hard and not run. You know that feeling. You've had it before. After the conversation you dreaded but finally had. After the step you almost didn't take. Every act of courage you've ever taken started with the exact same resistance you're feeling right now.

[15s pause]

There's a moment... right before courage happens... where everything in you says "not yet." And courage is simply deciding that "not yet" has had its turn. Now is now.

[10s pause]

Lord... I'm afraid. You know what I'm avoiding, even if I don't want to say it out loud. I don't feel brave today. But you commanded courage, which means you'll supply what the command requires. Go with me into the thing I want to run from. Not around it. Through it. Give me one breath of bravery. That's enough to start. Amen.

[5s pause]

One breath. One step. One honest word. That's all courage asks of you today.

You are commanded to be strong. Not because you are alone... but precisely because you are not. The God who goes with you will never leave you, will never forsake you, wherever you go.

[5s pause]

Carry that. And go with courage.
