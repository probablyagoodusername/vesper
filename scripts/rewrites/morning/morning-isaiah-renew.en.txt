[BREATHING: Slow diaphragmatic breathing — deep belly breaths, 5 counts in, 5 counts out, 4 cycles]

Good morning.

Start with a breath that goes all the way down. Not into your chest... into your belly. Let your stomach expand as the air fills. In for five... and out for five.

[5s pause]

Again. In... two... three... four... five. Out... two... three... four... five.

[5s pause]

Once more. Fill your belly... and release. Feel your body settle with the exhale.

[5s pause]

And one last time. In... and out.

[10s pause]

Good. Just be here for a moment. You don't have to be ready yet. You don't have to be anything yet. Let your body be heavy. Let the surface beneath you hold your weight. You are allowed to be tired right now.

[5s pause]

Maybe you are tired this morning. Maybe yesterday took more than it gave. Maybe you went to bed with something unresolved, and it followed you into today. Maybe you're running on fumes and wondering how you're going to get through another one. Maybe the weariness isn't even from yesterday... it's been building for weeks. Months.

If that's you... this passage was written for you. Specifically. Intentionally. For the weary.

[5s pause]

The prophet Isaiah, speaking to a people who had been worn down by exile, by decades of waiting, by the creeping suspicion that God had forgotten them, wrote this:

"He gives strength to the weary and increases the power of the weak. Even youths grow tired and weary, and young men stumble and fall... but those who hope in the Lord will renew their strength. They will soar on wings like eagles; they will run and not grow weary; they will walk and not be faint."

[10s pause]

Listen to who this promise is for. Not the already-strong. Not the ones who have it all together. Not the ones who bounced out of bed this morning full of energy. The weary. The weak. The ones who stumble. The ones who fall.

That might be you today. And if it is... you're exactly who God is speaking to.

[5s pause]

Strength is not something you manufacture. It's something you receive. And the source... has no limit. He doesn't get tired. He doesn't grow weary. His reserves don't run low. And he gives... not sells, not lends, not trades... gives... strength to the weary.

[10s pause]

Close your eyes if they aren't already. Imagine warmth starting at the center of your chest. Not heat... warmth. Like an ember catching light after a long, cold night. With each breath, it grows a little brighter.

[10s pause]

It spreads to your shoulders. Feel the tension there start to soften... not because you're forcing it, but because something stronger than your stress is moving through you. The warmth flows down your arms. Into your hands. Feel them ready. Open and close your fists once... feel the life in them. The grip. The capability.

[10s pause]

That warmth moves down through your core. Your hips. Your legs. Down into your feet. Press them into the ground... solid, stable, planted. Feel the surface beneath you pushing back. You are held.

[10s pause]

Now imagine standing tall. Feel your spine straighten... one vertebra at a time. Your shoulders draw back. Your chin lifts slightly. Not with tension... with quiet readiness. Like someone who knows they don't have to carry this alone. Like someone who just remembered where their strength actually comes from.

[10s pause]

Lord... I'm tired. You know how tired. I don't have what today requires, and I'm done pretending I do. I've been running on my own strength, and it's not enough. It was never supposed to be. So I'm asking you plainly... renew me. Not with adrenaline. Not with another burst of willpower that fades by noon. With your strength. The kind that carries the weary. The kind that lifts the ones who've stopped being able to lift themselves. I hope in you today. Not because I feel strong... but because you are. Amen.

[10s pause]

Three deep breaths now. Each one is an act of receiving. Not performing. Receiving.

Breathe in... strength for the weary... hold it at the top for a moment... and release what's been weighing you down.

[10s pause]

Breathe in... power for the weak... hold... and let go of the pressure to do this on your own.

[10s pause]

One more. Breathe in... renewal... hold... and exhale completely.

[10s pause]

Feel your feet on the ground again. Feel the steadiness that wasn't there five minutes ago. You're not dragging yourself through today. You are being carried by something larger than your own reserves. Something that doesn't run out.

[5s pause]

"Those who hope in the Lord will renew their strength. They will soar on wings like eagles; they will run and not grow weary; they will walk and not be faint."

[5s pause]

May that promise carry you today... through every meeting, every conversation, every moment your energy dips and the weariness creeps back in. The Lord who gives strength to the weary... is with you now. He was with you before you asked. And he will be with you long after this meditation ends.

Go in his renewal. And when you grow tired again... come back to this. He gives strength to the weary. Every time.
