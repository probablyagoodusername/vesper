[BREATHING: Box breathing — inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4, 4 cycles]

Good morning.

Let's slow your mind down before it speeds up.

We're going to breathe in a square. Four counts in, four counts holding, four counts out, four counts holding. Follow along.

Breathe in... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four. Breathe out... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four.

[3s pause]

Again. In... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four. Out... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four.

[3s pause]

Two more. In... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four. Out... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four.

[3s pause]

Last one. In... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four. Out... two... three... four. And hold... two... three... four.

[5s pause]

Good. Feel that. Your nervous system just downshifted. Your prefrontal cortex... the part of your brain that actually makes wise decisions... it just came back online. Stress shuts it down. Calm turns it on. That's not metaphor. That's neuroscience.

[5s pause]

Now. Here's what Solomon wrote three thousand years ago:

"Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight." Proverbs 3, verses 5 and 6.

Lean not on your own understanding. That's not an insult to your intelligence. It's an invitation to stop carrying the weight of omniscience. You don't have to figure everything out. You were never designed to.

[10s pause]

And then James, writing to early Christians scattered and stressed, said this:

"If any of you lacks wisdom, you should ask God, who gives generously to all without finding fault, and it will be given to you." James 1, verse 5.

Without finding fault. Hear that. God doesn't audit your track record before answering. He doesn't say "Well, last time you asked for wisdom, you ignored it, so..." No. Generously. To all. Without finding fault.

[10s pause]

So right now... think of a decision you're carrying. It might be something today. It might be something that's been circling for weeks. A conversation. A career question. A relationship. A boundary you need to set or release.

Name it quietly in your mind.

[15s pause]

Now notice what happens in your body when you hold that decision. Do your shoulders tighten? Does your breathing get shallow? Does your stomach clench? Just notice. Don't fix it. Just see where the weight lands.

[10s pause]

That tension is your body trying to solve the problem through force. Through analysis. Through control. And sometimes analysis helps. But wisdom is different from analysis. Analysis asks "What are all the options?" Wisdom asks "What is the next right thing?"

[10s pause]

So set aside the noise for a moment. The opinions. The fears. The pressure to decide perfectly. And ask simply... what does your conscience tell you? Not your anxiety. Not the voice of someone else's expectation. That quiet, steady knowing underneath the noise.

[15s pause]

If the answer is clear... trust it. If it's not clear yet... that's okay too. James says wisdom will be given. Not always on your timeline. But given. Sometimes wisdom sounds like "wait." And waiting is not the same as avoiding.

[10s pause]

Now unclench your hands. Literally. Open your fists if they were closed. Spread your fingers. Feel the release. Tight hands, tight mind. Open hands, open to receive. Wisdom arrives better in open hands than clenched ones.

[10s pause]

Take one more breath. Slow. Full. And as you exhale, release the need to have it all figured out by noon. Wisdom is patient work. It doesn't panic.

[10s pause]

Lord... I need wisdom today. Not the kind I can manufacture by overthinking. The kind only you give. You said you'd give it generously, without finding fault. So I'm asking. For the decision I just named, and for the ones I haven't seen coming yet. Quiet the noise. Steady my judgment. Help me trust that I don't need to see the whole road... just the next step. Amen.

[5s pause]

One last thing. Proverbs says God will make your paths straight. Not that he'll show you the map. The path becomes clear by walking, not by staring at the horizon.

[5s pause]

So today, walk with your eyes open and your hands unclenched. Make the next wise choice in front of you. And then the next one. That's how wisdom works... not in one grand revelation, but in a thousand small faithfulnesses.

[5s pause]

May God give you clarity when you need it and peace when clarity hasn't arrived yet.

Amen.
