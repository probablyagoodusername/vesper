Good morning.

Before you think about what you need to do today... let's start with who you are.

Breathe with me. In through your nose for four... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four... five... six... seven. And exhale slowly through your mouth... two... three... four... five... six... seven... eight.

[3s pause]

Again. In... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four... five... six... seven. Exhale... two... three... four... five... six... seven... eight.

[3s pause]

One more. In... two... three... four. Hold... two... three... four... five... six... seven. And release... two... three... four... five... six... seven... eight.

[5s pause]

Good. Feel your body settle. Notice the ground beneath you... the surface supporting your weight. You don't have to hold yourself up right now. You're held.

[5s pause]

Here's Paul, writing to the church in Ephesus:

"For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do." Ephesians 2, verse 10.

Handiwork. The Greek word is poiema... it's where we get the English word "poem." You are not a product. Not an output. A poem. Something crafted with intention, shaped with care, meant to say something that nothing else says.

[10s pause]

And then there's Jeremiah 29:11... a verse you've probably seen on coffee mugs and dorm room posters. "For I know the plans I have for you, declares the Lord, plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future."

Here's what the coffee mugs leave out. God spoke those words to an entire nation living in exile. In Babylon. Displaced, defeated, grieving. They'd lost their homes, their temple, everything familiar. And God said: even here... I have plans. Not after exile. During exile. The promise isn't that life will be comfortable. It's that God is working purpose even in displacement.

[10s pause]

So if your life doesn't look the way you thought it would... if you feel like you're in a season of exile, or waiting, or confusion... that doesn't disqualify you from purpose. Jeremiah's audience was living proof.

[10s pause]

Now let's bring this into your body. Press your fingertips together... all ten, like a tent. Feel the pressure points. Each finger alive, sensitive, capable. These hands will do something today. They'll type, or hold, or build, or comfort, or create. That's not nothing. That's the good work Ephesians is talking about.

[10s pause]

Purpose isn't always a grand calling. Most days, it's small and specific. The way you listen when someone needs to be heard. The care you bring to work that no one notices. The patience you offer when it costs you something. The meal you make. The message you send. The standard you hold.

[10s pause]

Let's sit with this gently. Not "What is my one cosmic purpose?"... that question is too big for a Tuesday morning. Instead: what good work is right in front of me today? Who might I serve? Where might I bring something... even something small... that only I can bring?

[15s pause]

Maybe you know. Maybe it's already obvious... your job, your family, your craft. Or maybe purpose today is quieter. Being present. Showing up honestly. Not performing, just being faithful in the small things.

[10s pause]

Now release your fingertips and rest your hands, palms up, on your lap or at your sides. Open hands. Ready to receive the work and ready to do it. Not grasping. Not striving. Open.

[10s pause]

There's a freedom in realizing that purpose isn't something you have to invent. Ephesians says it was prepared in advance. It's already there. Your job isn't to create purpose out of thin air. It's to show up with enough presence and willingness to recognize it when it's in front of you.

[10s pause]

Lord... I don't always feel purposeful. Some mornings I feel more like a rough draft than a poem. But you said you made me as handiwork... crafted, intentional, not accidental. Help me see the good work you've prepared for today. Not the grand plan. Just today's work. Give me eyes to notice it and hands willing to do it. Even if it's small. Even if no one sees. Amen.

[5s pause]

You are God's handiwork. Created with intention. Placed in this exact moment for a reason.

The good work prepared for you today is real. It's waiting. And you don't have to see the whole blueprint to do the next faithful thing.

[5s pause]

Go... not to perform, but to be present. The poem is still being written.
