James, writing to early Christians scattered by persecution... people who had lost homes, safety, community... wrote this:

"Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds, because you know that the testing of your faith produces perseverance. Let perseverance finish its work so that you may be mature and complete, not lacking anything." James 1, verses 2 through 4.

Consider it joy. That's a strange thing to say to people who are suffering. But notice... James doesn't say the trial is joyful. He says consider it joy... because of what it produces. There's a chain here. Trial produces perseverance. Perseverance produces maturity. Maturity produces completeness. Each link forged under pressure.

[10s pause]

Not masochistic joy. Not "smile through the pain." This is the settled recognition that difficulty is doing something in you... not just to you.

[10s pause]

And then Romans 8, verse 28: "We know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose."

Not "all things are good." Paul is not saying your suffering was a gift. He's saying God's capacity to bring good from wreckage is beyond what we can see in the moment. Some things just hurt. Full stop. And God works within that hurt.
