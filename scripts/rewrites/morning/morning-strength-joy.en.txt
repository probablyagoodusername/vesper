Good morning.

"The joy of the Lord is your strength." Nehemiah 8:10.

Not your coffee. Not your willpower. Not your to-do list. The joy of the Lord. That's your strength today.

Let's wake your body up.

Quick, sharp breath in through your nose... short exhale. In... out. In... out. In... out. In... out. In... out. In... out.

[5s pause]

Now a slow, deep breath in through your nose... fill your whole chest... and let it all go.

[10s pause]

Good. You can feel the shift. Your blood is moving. Your lungs are open. Your body is waking up with you. That change you feel... that alertness... it happened in seconds. One breathing pattern and your whole physiology responded. Remember that. You are not stuck in whatever state you wake up in.

[5s pause]

Stretch your arms overhead. Reach as high as you can. Feel your spine lengthen, your ribcage expand. Hold that stretch for a moment... reaching... reaching... and release.

[10s pause]

Now roll your shoulders back... one... two... three times. And let them settle down and back. Feel the openness across your chest. That's the posture of someone who isn't bracing for the day... but stepping into it.

[5s pause]

Unclench your jaw. Soften your face. Let your tongue drop from the roof of your mouth. Relax the muscles around your eyes. So much tension lives in the face without us knowing it.

[5s pause]

Now press your feet into the floor. Feel the ground under you. Solid. Stable. Push down slightly and feel the floor push back. You're here. You're awake. And you're awake for a reason.

[5s pause]

The context of this verse matters. Nehemiah's people had just heard the Law of God read aloud for the first time in generations. They were weeping... overwhelmed by how far they'd fallen, how much they'd lost, how long they'd been disconnected from who they were meant to be. And Nehemiah said... don't mourn. Don't grieve. This is a sacred day. "The joy of the Lord is your strength."

Not happiness. Happiness depends on what happens. Joy is something deeper. It's the kind that comes from knowing you belong to something unshakable, even when everything around you shakes. It's the quiet confidence that you are known, you are held, and today has purpose... even if you can't see all of it yet.

[10s pause]

So here's the question this morning: where are you trying to manufacture your own strength?

Where are you white-knuckling your way through... running on discipline and caffeine and sheer stubbornness... when what you actually need is to let joy back in?

[10s pause]

Maybe it's a relationship that's draining you. Maybe it's a project that feels impossible. Maybe it's the general weight of responsibility that never seems to lighten. Where are you carrying something that was never meant to be carried alone?

[15s pause]

This is the invitation: stop manufacturing. Start receiving. Not passivity... partnership. Your effort matters. But it was never meant to be the foundation. Joy is.

[5s pause]

Lord... I confess that I try to be my own source most days. I reach for control before I reach for you. I mistake exhaustion for dedication, and stubbornness for strength. Today, I want something different. I want to draw from your joy instead of my reserves. Teach me the difference between striving and trusting. And when I forget... which I will... bring me back to this: the joy of the Lord is my strength. Not mine. Yours. Amen.

[15s pause]

See yourself in the hard moment... the one you're already dreading a little... and instead of bracing, you breathe. You remember. Joy. Not a feeling you have to produce. A gift you have to receive.

[15s pause]

Now see yourself at the end of the day. Tired, maybe. But not depleted. Because you drew from a source that doesn't run dry.
