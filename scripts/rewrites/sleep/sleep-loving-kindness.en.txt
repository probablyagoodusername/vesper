Tonight we practice a form of prayer that has roots in both contemplative Christian tradition and ancient Buddhist practice — loving-kindness. In some traditions it's called metta. In Christian contemplative prayer, it echoes the great commandment Jesus repeated more than any other: love one another. Love your neighbor as yourself. Love even your enemies.

We'll move through widening circles of compassion — starting with yourself, then someone you love, then someone neutral, then someone difficult, and finally... everyone. All beings. The whole broken, beautiful world.

The apostle John writes: "There is no fear in love. Perfect love casts out fear." First John, chapter four, verse eighteen.

Fear and love occupy the same territory in the body — the tight chest, the clenched hands, the guarded heart. Tonight, we let love expand into those places where fear has been living. Not by force. By invitation.
