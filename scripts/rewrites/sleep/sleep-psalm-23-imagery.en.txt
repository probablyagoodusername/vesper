[BREATHING: Sleep Preparation pattern — 4-count inhale through nose, 8-count exhale through mouth, 3-count hold at bottom. Maintain throughout opening settling. Voice is low, unhurried, warm.]

Welcome to rest. There's nothing you need to do right now... nothing you need to figure out... nothing you need to fix or finish. Just be where you are.

Take a moment to adjust your position. Find the way your body wants to lie tonight. Maybe shift your shoulders a little... rock your hips from side to side until they settle... let your head find its place on the pillow. There's no perfect position. There's just the one where your body says... okay... I can stop adjusting now.

[10s pause]

Now notice where your body meets the bed. Start with the back of your head... feel the weight of your skull resting down. Your shoulder blades... two broad points pressing gently into the mattress. The curve of your lower back. The heaviness of your hips. The backs of your thighs. The calves. The heels of your feet.

[10s pause]

Each of these points of contact is a place where you can stop holding yourself up. The bed is doing that work. It's been doing it all along. You've just been too alert to notice. Notice it now. Let the bed hold you completely.

[15s pause]

Bring your attention to your breathing. Don't change it yet... just notice the rhythm that's already there. The quiet rise of the chest... the gentle fall. The way the body breathes itself, whether you pay attention or not. It's been doing this all day without you thinking about it. It will keep going tonight.

[10s pause]

Now, gently begin to lengthen your exhale. Breathe in through your nose for a count of four... one... two... three... four. And out through your mouth, slowly, for eight... one... two... three... four... five... six... seven... eight. Feel the breath thin out at the end, like a thread of smoke dissolving.

[10s pause]

Again. In... two... three... four. And out... long and slow... letting the breath carry everything you don't need out of your body... two... three... four... five... six... seven... eight.

[10s pause]

One more. In... filling the lungs gently... and out... completely... all the way to empty. Good. Let your breathing find its own pace now. You've set it in motion. It knows what to do.

[15s pause]

Tonight, we're going to walk through the most beloved psalm ever written. Twenty-three. And as we walk through it, we'll let it do what it was written to do... quiet us down... settle us into the care of God... and lead us, gently, toward sleep.

"The Lord is my shepherd. I shall not want."

[15s pause]

Let those words rest in you. Not as something to analyze or theologize about... but as a statement about tonight. Right now, in this bed, in this room, with whatever is on your mind... you are watched over. You are tended to. You lack nothing that you need for this exact moment. The moment of lying down.

[15s pause]

"He maketh me to lie down in green pastures."

He maketh you to lie down. Not suggests. Not offers. Not asks politely. He maketh you. There is a firmness to this kindness. A shepherd knows the sheep won't rest on their own. They'll keep grazing, keep moving, keep watching for threats, even when they're exhausted. So the shepherd leads them to a place where lying down is the only thing left to do. That's where you are tonight. You have permission to stop striving. More than permission. You are being led to rest by someone who knows you need it more than you do.

[15s pause]

And so we begin there. Lying down. Letting the body release, group by group, the way a field releases the heat of the day after the sun goes down.

Bring your attention to your feet. Gently curl your toes... squeeze the muscles in both feet, the arches, the soles... hold them tight for five seconds. One... two... three... four... five. And release. Let them go completely. Feel the muscles unknot... feel the warmth that floods in where the tension was. Your feet resting on cool, thick grass. The kind of grass that gives beneath you... soft and damp at the roots.

[15s pause]

Now your calves. Tighten the muscles in both calves... pull your toes up toward your shins to engage them fully. Hold that tension. One... two... three... four... five. And let go. Feel them soften and spread against the mattress. Heavy and warm... like they're resting in sun-warmed grass, the blades soft against your skin, the earth solid and cool beneath.

[15s pause]

Your thighs. Squeeze the muscles in both thighs... press your legs down firmly into the bed. Feel the tension run from your knees to your hips. Hold. One... two... three... four... five. And release. Feel the heaviness pour down through your legs like warm water. All the standing and walking and climbing of the day... all that effort... draining into the mattress, draining into the earth beneath the green pastures.

[15s pause]

Move to your hips and your lower back. Gently press your lower back into the mattress... tighten your glutes... feel the whole base of your body engage. Hold. One... two... three... four... five. And let go. Feel your pelvis settle... your lower back widen and flatten against the bed. The earth beneath you, ancient and patient, holding your weight without complaint.

[15s pause]

Your stomach and your chest. Take a deep breath in and tighten your abdomen... pull everything in toward your spine, squeeze your ribs together. Hold it. One... two... three... four... five. And release with a long exhale. Feel your belly soften completely. Feel your ribs open and expand outward. There's space here now... space to breathe deeply... space to let the lungs do their easy, automatic work.

[15s pause]

Now your hands. Make two fists. Squeeze them tight... fingernails pressing into your palms, tendons standing out along the backs of your hands. Hold. One... two... three... four... five. And open them. Let your fingers uncurl slowly, one by one... let your palms face upward or downward, wherever they fall. Feel the blood rushing back into your fingers, a gentle tingling, a warmth spreading through your open hands. Your hands have nothing to grip tonight. Nothing to type, nothing to carry, nothing to hold onto.

[15s pause]

Your forearms and your upper arms. Bend your elbows and flex both arms, tensing everything from wrist to shoulder. Hold it all tight. One... two... three... four... five. And drop them. Let your arms fall heavy at your sides like they're made of warm clay. Feel them sink into the bed... warm... loose... finished with effort for the day.

[15s pause]

Your shoulders. This is where the day hides. This is where you've been carrying things you didn't even know you were carrying. Lift your shoulders up toward your ears... high as they'll go... feel the tension bunch at the top. Hold them there. One... two... three... four... five. And let them fall. Not lower them... let them fall. Feel the distance between your ears and your shoulders grow. Feel the neck lengthen. That's the release. That's the day leaving your body.

[15s pause]

And finally, your face. Scrunch everything up... forehead, eyes, nose, mouth, jaw... squeeze your whole face toward the center, as tight as you can. Hold it. One... two... three... four... five. And let go. Feel your forehead smooth out like water after a stone drops in. Your eyelids soften. Your jaw drops slightly open. The muscles around your eyes release their grip. Your tongue settles at the bottom of your mouth, loose and warm. Your cheeks rest. Your lips part gently.

[15s pause]

Your whole body now... from the soles of your feet to the crown of your head... has been invited to let go. And it has accepted. You are lying down in green pastures. This is what the psalm describes. Not a metaphor tonight... a physical reality. Your body is at rest in a place of safety.

[20s pause]

"He leadeth me beside the still waters."

[10s pause]

See it now. Let the picture form slowly, the way an image develops in a dark room. A stream. Barely moving. The water so calm that the surface reflects the sky above it like a mirror. Silver and pale gold in the fading light.

[15s pause]

The stream is narrow enough that you could step across it. The banks are low, covered in thick grass and clover. The water moves over smooth stones on the bottom... stones that have been here longer than anyone can remember... worn round and flat by centuries of patient current. You can hear it... a sound so quiet you have to stop everything else to catch it. The soft, continuous murmur of water over stone.

[15s pause]

You're sitting beside this stream, though sitting is generous... you're more lying than sitting now, propped on one elbow, then giving in to the slope of the bank and lying back. The grass beneath you is dense and cool, the kind that tickles the backs of your arms. Each blade distinct. The earth underneath is firm but gives slightly, like it's making room for you.

[15s pause]

The air has that quality it gets in the last hour before dark. Still, but not stagnant. Cool on your skin but not cold. It carries scent... green things growing, the mineral smell of clean water, wild herbs at the water's edge. Mint, maybe. Or something like thyme... earthy and clean. You breathe it in and it goes deep, not just into your lungs but into some part of you that needed exactly this.

[15s pause]

The light is changing. The gold is deepening toward amber, then copper. The shadows of the trees along the far bank stretch long across the water. A single bird calls from somewhere behind you... a clear, unhurried note... and then settles into silence. Even the bird is done with the day.

[20s pause]

Listen to the water. That's all. Just the water and the silence around it. The stream doesn't hurry. It doesn't worry about where it's going. It just moves, steady and sure, toward whatever comes next.

[20s pause]

"He restoreth my soul."

With each breath, feel yourself being restored. Not by your own effort... not by willpower or discipline or positive thinking. By presence. The way a field restores itself when you stop plowing it. The way water clears when you stop stirring it. The way a wound heals when you stop touching it. You don't have to fix anything tonight. You are being restored by the One who made you... who knows how you work... who designed rest for exactly this purpose.

[20s pause]

"Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for thou art with me."

[10s pause]

Whatever shadows followed you today... whatever worry or regret or unnamed dread came to bed with you tonight... hear this. The psalm does not say there are no valleys. It does not say the shadows aren't real. It does not promise that you will never walk through dark places. It says something more honest and more powerful than that. It says... you are not alone in them. You are accompanied through the dark. That is a different thing entirely from pretending the dark is not there.

[20s pause]

"Thy rod and thy staff, they comfort me."

The rod to protect. The staff to guide. A shepherd's tools, designed not to punish but to keep the sheep from wandering into danger. Feel that comfort now. Not as an abstract idea... but as something close. Like a hand resting on your shoulder in a dark room. Like a voice nearby that doesn't need to say much... just... I am here. I know the way through this valley. Stay close. You can close your eyes. I'll watch.

[20s pause]

"Thou preparest a table before me in the presence of mine enemies."

Even now... even with whatever troubles are out there circling... a table has been set. There is provision. There is enough. Not because the enemies have gone away... but because the host is greater. You can eat at a table set in the open, in full view of everything that threatens, because the One who set it is not worried about what's watching.

[15s pause]

"Thou anointest my head with oil. My cup runneth over."

Abundance. Not scarcity. Not barely enough. More than enough. The cup overflows. Tonight, you are not running on empty. You are being filled.

[15s pause]

"Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life."

Not chase you. Follow you. Goodness and mercy are not running you down. They are walking behind you, quiet and steady... guarding the path you've already traveled... covering the missteps... filling in the low places. Every day you've lived, they were there. Every day to come, they will be. They don't depend on your performance. They follow because that is their nature.

[20s pause]

"And I will dwell in the house of the Lord... forever."

[15s pause]

Lord... thank you for this night. Thank you for the green pastures you lead us to, even when we resist the resting. Thank you for the still waters that ask nothing of us. Quiet what is loud in us tonight. Restore what the day has worn down. We trust you with the hours we can't see through. Amen.

[30s pause]

You are lying in green pastures now. The stream is beside you... its sound so steady it has become part of the silence, the way a heartbeat becomes part of sleep. The air is cool on your skin. The grass is soft beneath your body. The sky above you has turned deep blue, then deeper, and now the first stars are appearing... faint, then clear, then bright.

[20s pause]

The light is almost gone. Everything is blue and quiet and still. Your body is heavy against the earth. Your breathing is slow and even. The shepherd is near. You can't see him... but you don't need to. You can feel his presence the way you feel the ground beneath you... constant, unhurried, sure.

[30s pause]

The water moves over the stones. The grass holds you. Your eyes are closed. Your hands are open. Goodness is behind you. Mercy is beside you. The shepherd watches.

[30s pause]

Green and quiet... the water... the grass... the cool air... the stars appearing one by one.

[30s pause]

The pasture holds you. Sleep comes the way night comes... not all at once... but gently... and then completely.
